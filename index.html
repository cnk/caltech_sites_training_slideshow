Why do you need a website?

Possible reasons for you or your group to have a web site:

  • To tell other people at Caltech about the services you provide to the campus (example)
  • To attract new people to Caltech (example)
  • To tell the world about your research (example)

Before you start building your site, think about your audience(s) and what you want them to know after visiting your site.

What should your site say?

Building a web site is a lot like writing a term paper. Start by making an outline of what you need to say. Look at examples of sites that are similar to yours and are doing an effective job displaying the information you need to convey. How have they organized their site?

Start basic. Write and a brief introduction to your office / lab / project. Then start working your way through your outline.

If it helps you to create the entire outline first, feel free to do so - just don't put the unfinished pages in your site's navigation. That draws attention to what is missing and makes the content that is there look less reliable.
